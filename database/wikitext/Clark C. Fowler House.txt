The Clark C. Fowler House near Tompkinsville, Kentucky is an I-house which was built in 1880.  It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2001.It is a two-story frame, central passage plan structure, with Queen Anne influence in details of dormers and its rear porch.  It was built to serve as a boardinghouse and is on a hillside overlooking the Cumberland River.  It is the only surviving dwelling in the riverside community of Martinsburg.
