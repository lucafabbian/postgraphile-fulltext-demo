Winchester Town Hall is a historic town hall at 71 Mount Vernon Street in Winchester, Massachusetts.  The 2+1‚ÅÑ2-story brick building was built in 1887 to a design by Rand and Taylor.  It was funded in part by a bequest from William Parsons Winchester, for whom the town is named.  Stylistically the building has Queen Anne and Romanesque Revival styling.  Its most prominent feature is the clock tower, a four-plus story square tower topped by a pointed roof with gables.The building was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1983, and included in the Winchester Center Historic District in 1986.
