Dikhololo is a wildlife reserve in South Africa's North West Province, 35 kilometers (22 miles) outside of Brits. Dikhololo, a game farm and family-friendly resort, offers a bush experience. Visitors can get up close and personal with giraffes, a variety of deer, zebras, jackals, hundreds of bird species, and more. It features a variety of amenities and entertainment programs.
It is a publicly accessible resort, providing lodging in chalets grouped into camps throughout the reserve, some between low hills.
Animals that can be seen in the reserve include various antelope: Burchell's zebra, giraffe, common duiker, steenbok, blesbok, impala, blue wildebeest, red hartebeest, waterbuck, nyala, kudu and eland and other wildlife.
Various recreational facilities exist, from swimming pools (one heated) through water slides, flood-lit tennis courts, putt-putt, quad biking and game drives.
The resort gets visitors from the surrounding areas or nearby Pretoria and Johannesburg.
